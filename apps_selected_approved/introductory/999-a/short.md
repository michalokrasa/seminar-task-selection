Mishka can only attempt problems from the leftmost or rightmost end of a list, and he cannot solve any problem with difficulty greater than his skill level k. Each solved problem is removed from the list. Mishka stops as soon as both ends are too hard for him, and the task is to count how many problems he solves in total. The algorithmic approach is a two-pointer simulation advancing from either end; the main challenge is correctly detecting the stopping condition when both the current left and right endpoints simultaneously exceed k.
