Given a current time in 24-hour HH:MM format and a non-negative integer a (up to 10,000), output the time exactly a minutes later. The result must wrap correctly past midnight and be printed with two digits for both hours and minutes (with leading zeroes where needed). The algorithmic approach is modular arithmetic: convert to total minutes, add a, take modulo 1440 (minutes in a day), then extract hours and minutes; the main challenge is ensuring correct two-digit output formatting with leading zeroes.
