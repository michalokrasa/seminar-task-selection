Two players (Conan goes first) alternate turns picking a card from a shared set. Choosing a card removes it along with all cards bearing a strictly smaller number. The player who faces an empty set on their turn loses. Given the multiset of card values, determine who wins when both players play optimally. The algorithmic approach relies on a key game-theory insight: if there are multiple distinct values, Conan always wins by picking the maximum card (clearing everything at once); if all values are identical, the game reduces to taking one card per turn, so the first player wins if and only if the count is odd. The main challenge is deriving this insight rather than implementing full minimax search.
