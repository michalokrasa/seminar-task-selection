A broken clock shows a time in HH:MM format that may be invalid for either the 12-hour (01:00–12:59) or 24-hour (00:00–23:59) format specified in the input. The task is to find the closest valid time by changing the fewest individual digits. If multiple solutions tie on the number of changed digits, any of them is acceptable. The algorithmic approach is brute-force enumeration over all valid times in the given format, comparing digit-by-digit against the displayed time; the main challenge is correctly enumerating the 12-hour range (where hours run from 01 to 12, not 00 to 11) without missing valid times.
