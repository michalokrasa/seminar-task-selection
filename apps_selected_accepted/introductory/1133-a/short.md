Given the start and end times of a programming contest in HH:MM format, compute and output the exact midpoint time. The contest is guaranteed to last an even number of minutes and to fit within a single day, so the midpoint is always a whole minute. The result must be printed with leading zeroes (e.g. 02:05). The algorithmic approach is straightforward time arithmetic: convert both times to total minutes, compute the average, and convert back; the main challenge is correctly formatting the output with leading zeroes.
