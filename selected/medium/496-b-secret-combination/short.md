A combination lock displays n digits and has two operations: add 1 to all digits (with wrap-around 9→0), or rotate all digits one position right. Find the sequence of operations that produces the lexicographically smallest possible number (ignoring leading zeros for comparison). Since there are only 10 possible "add" counts (0–9), the solution tries all 10 offsets, applies each, then finds the best rotation for that offset, and picks the overall minimum.
