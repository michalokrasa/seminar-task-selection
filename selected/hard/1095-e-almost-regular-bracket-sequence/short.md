Given a bracket sequence of length n, count how many single-bracket flips (changing '(' to ')' or vice versa) result in a valid regular bracket sequence. A regular bracket sequence is one that forms a balanced expression. The sequence can be up to 10^6 characters long, requiring an efficient O(n) approach using prefix sums of bracket balance. The key insight is that flipping a bracket is valid only under specific conditions on the prefix and suffix balances around that position.
